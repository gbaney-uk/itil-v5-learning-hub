Exam Takeaways

Short points to remember before answering exam-style questions.

Four Dimensions Overview

  • The Four Dimensions provide a balanced, holistic perspective for managing products and services.
  • They should be considered together, not separately.
  • They support service relationships, governance, the ITIL value system, the service value chain, management practices, value streams and continual improvement.

Organisations and People

  • This dimension is not just about HR.
  • It includes leadership, culture, safety culture, structure, governance, roles, skills and collaboration.
  • Even strong technology or processes can fail if people factors are not aligned.

Information and Technology

  • This dimension is not just about tools.
  • It includes data, information, knowledge, governance, automation, AI, security, reliability and compliance.
  • Technology only creates value when information is accurate, accessible, protected, governed and used well.

Partners and Suppliers

  • This dimension is not just vendor management.
  • It covers sourcing, contracts, collaboration, dependencies, risk and value contribution.
  • External relationships should strengthen resilience, service outcomes and value creation.

Value Streams and Processes

  • Value streams show how value is created from start to finish.
  • Processes describe specific activities performed within that flow.
  • This dimension helps identify bottlenecks, handoffs, delays and improvement opportunities.

External Factors PESTLE

  • External Factors help organisations understand influences outside their direct control.
  • PESTLE stands for Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal and Environmental.
  • External factors should be considered when designing, changing, improving and assessing risk.

Practice Questions

Use these for active recall. Try to answer before revealing the answer.

Question 1

Why should the Four Dimensions be considered together?

Because focusing on one dimension in isolation can create gaps, risks, inefficiencies or poor decisions. The dimensions are interdependent and together give a balanced view of product and service management.

Question 2

What is the main idea behind Organisations and People?

Products and services depend on people factors such as leadership, culture, communication, structure, governance, roles, skills and collaboration. Good technology or processes can still fail if people factors are not aligned.

Question 3

Why is Information and Technology not just about tools?

Because it also includes the data, information, knowledge, governance, automation, AI, security, reliability and compliance needed to support products and services.

Question 4

What does Partners and Suppliers cover beyond simple procurement?

It covers sourcing choices, contracts, collaboration, dependencies, supplier integration, risk, resilience and how external organisations contribute to value creation.

Question 5

What is the difference between a value stream and a process?

A value stream shows the end-to-end flow of how value is created. A process describes specific activities or steps that may happen within that value stream.

Question 6

What does PESTLE stand for?

Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal and Environmental.
